Li Lab

Our laboratory sits at the intersection of clinical dermatology and molecular biology. We decode the pathogenic mechanisms of pilosebaceous-related diseases — including rosacea, hair loss, and hair graying — and investigate the fundamental processes of skin aging. By integrating patient cohorts, multi-omics profiling, disease models, and translational experiments, we develop targeted therapies that bridge bench discovery with bedside application.

High-impact discoveries published in Cell, Nature Medicine, Nature Communications, Science Advances, and JAAD have advanced our understanding from molecular mechanisms to clinical interventions across both research directions.

Prof. Ji Li

Director of Li Lab

Pilosebaceous-Related Diseases

  • Identified α-ketoglutarate as a rosacea metabolite that activates OXGR1; developed the selective agonist A-1 for erythema treatment (Cell 2026).
  • Single-cell atlas revealed CTS hypercontractility–PIEZO1 axis driving androgenetic alopecia; MLCK inhibitor ML-7 restores hair growth (Nat Commun 2026).
  • Established neuro-immune CGRP–RAMP1 circuit and pro-inflammatory fibroblast PTGDS pathway in rosacea (Nat Commun 2024; Nat Commun 2024).
  • Hedgehog-driven SCUBE3 signaling induces de novo hair growth from dermal papilla niche (Dev Cell 2022).

Skin Aging

  • Core member of the national Aging Biomarker Consortium establishing standardized aging biomarkers across organ systems (Nat Med 2023).
  • Longevity cohort transcriptomics revealed ETS1 governs healthy aging by downregulating ribosomal biogenesis as an adaptive energy-saving mechanism (Sci Adv 2022).
  • FTO depletion drives heterochromatin loss via KAT8/RSF1 cascade, linking RNA m6A modification to chromatin remodeling in skin aging (MedComm 2025).
  • HOXC10 delays fibroblast senescence via FZD6/Wnt/β-catenin; simvastatin identified as functional mimic (Research 2025).
