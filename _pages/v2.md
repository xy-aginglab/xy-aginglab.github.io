    <div class="v2-about__mission">
      <p>Our laboratory bridges clinical dermatology with basic and translational science. Our research centers on three major directions: <em style="font-style:normal;font-weight:700;">rosacea &amp; hidradenitis suppurativa</em>, <em style="font-style:normal;font-weight:700;">hair disorders &amp; regeneration</em>, and <em style="font-style:normal;font-weight:700;">skin aging</em>. We combine clinical practice with clinical research on disease patterns, natural history, and therapeutic outcomes, while also conducting mechanistic studies grounded in rich patient resources. Through patient cohorts, multi-omics approaches, disease models, and translational studies, we aim to define disease pathogenesis and advance targeted therapeutic strategies, including clinical trials of novel treatments and drug candidates.</p>
      <p>Together, these efforts reflect our commitment to integrating clinical insight, biological discovery, and therapeutic innovation to advance precision dermatology.</p>
      <p>Our work, published in <em>Cell</em>, <em>BMJ</em>, <em>Nature Medicine</em>, <em>Nature Communications</em>, <em>Developmental Cell</em>, <em>Science Advances</em>, <em>JAMA Dermatology</em>, <em>JAAD</em> and <em>JID</em>, has contributed to progress from mechanistic discovery to clinical translation across these research areas.</p>
